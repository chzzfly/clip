People often say “knowledge is power.” But what happens when we all have different levels of knowledge?  
人们常说 "知识就是力量"。但是，当我们都有不同的知识水平时会发生什么？

The “curse of knowledge,” or “the curse of expertise,” is a cognitive bias where we incorrectly assume that everyone knows as much as we do on a given topic.<sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">1</sup> When we know something, it can be hard to imagine what it would be like not knowing that piece of information. In turn, this makes it difficult to share our knowledge, because we struggle to understand the other party’s state of mind.  
知识的诅咒 "或 "专业知识的诅咒 "是一种认知偏见，即我们错误地认为每个人都和我们一样对某一特定主题了如指掌。 <sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">1</sup> 当我们知道某件事情时，可能很难想象不知道这条信息会是什么样子。反过来，这使得我们很难分享我们的知识，因为我们很难理解对方的思想状态。

This is a facet of learning that The Decision Lab hopes to address. Many people with interest in behavioral science come from different backgrounds and levels of familiarity. Our goal is to make information as digestible as possible: to do that, we need to avoid the curse of knowledge!

## History

In 1975, American psychologist Baruch Fischhoff published, “Hindsight ≠ foresight: The effect of outcome knowledge on judgement under uncertainty.”<sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">2</sup> Fischhoff developed a method to examine **[hindsight bias](https://thedecisionlab.com/biases/hindsight-bias)**, a cognitive bias where an event’s outcome seems more predictable after we know what happened.  
1975年，美国心理学家Baruch Fischhoff发表了 "事后诸葛亮≠事前诸葛亮。结果知识对不确定性下判断的影响"。0# Fischhoff开发了一种方法来研究事后诸葛亮的偏见，这是一种认知偏见，即在我们知道发生了什么之后，事件的结果似乎更可预测。

Fischhoff’s method consisted of presenting participants with four possible outcomes to a short story.<sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">2</sup> Some participants were told which one of the four outcomes was true; other participants were not given any information. Then, all participants were asked to determine the likelihood of each outcome. Fischhoff found that, when a participant was told an outcome was true, they frequently assigned a higher probability to that outcome. On top of overestimating the probability of outcomes for which they had extra information, participants also failed to reconstruct their prior, less knowledgeable states of mind.  
Fischhoff的方法包括向参与者展示一个小故事的四个可能结果。 <sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">2</sup> 一些参与者被告知这四种结果中的哪一种是真的；其他参与者没有得到任何信息。然后，所有参与者被要求确定每种结果的可能性。Fischhoff发现，当参与者被告知某个结果是真的时候，他们经常给这个结果分配更高的概率。除了高估他们拥有额外信息的结果的概率外，参与者也未能重建他们之前的、知识较少的心态。

Stemming from Fischoff’s work on hindsight bias, the term “curse of knowledge” was first used in the 1989 article “The curse of knowledge in economic settings: An experimental analysis” by economists Colin Camerer, George Loewenstein, and Martin Weber.<sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">3</sup> They credited British-American psychologist Robin Hogarth with coining the term, and they explored the curse of knowledge in the context of economic transactions.  
源于Fischoff关于事后诸葛亮的工作，"知识的诅咒 "一词首次在1989年的文章《经济环境中知识的诅咒》中使用。经济学家Colin Camerer、George Loewenstein和Martin Weber在1989年的文章《经济环境中的知识诅咒：实验分析》中首次使用。他们认为是英美心理学家罗宾-霍加斯创造了这个词，他们在经济交易的背景下探讨了知识的诅咒。

Their study observed that different economic agents have different amounts of knowledge.<sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">3</sup> Sellers tend to know more about the value of their products than prospective buyers; workers tend to know more about their skills than prospective employers. Crucially, Camerer, Loewenstein, and Weber argued that the curse of knowledge perpetuates this informational imbalance even when an agent _wants_ to convey what they know. They also argued that this unintentional imbalance had two consequences:  
他们的研究观察到，不同的经济主体拥有不同数量的知识。卖家往往比潜在的买家更了解他们产品的价值；工人往往比潜在的雇主更了解他们的技能。最重要的是，Camerer、Loewenstein和Weber认为，知识的诅咒使这种信息的不平衡永久化，即使代理人想要传达他们所知道的信息。他们还认为，这种无意的不平衡有两个后果。

1.  Better-informed agents might suffer losses – having more information might hurt us!  
    信息更灵通的代理人可能会遭受损失--拥有更多的信息可能会伤害到我们!
2.  The curse of knowledge can mitigate market consequences that result from information asymmetry. A fruit seller might lower prices to reflect unobservable defects.  
    知识的诅咒可以减轻信息不对称造成的市场后果。一个卖水果的人可能会降低价格以反映不可观察的缺陷。

Following Camerer, Loewenstein, and Weber’s work, Elizabeth Newton, a graduate student in psychology at Stanford in 1990, developed an experiment that is now a classic example of the curse of knowledge.<sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">1 4</sup> She asked participants to tap out popular songs with their fingers (known as the “tappers”), before predicting how many of those tapped melodies would be recognized by other people (known as the “listeners”). She also asked the tappers to predict how many people would guess the melody correctly.  
继Camerer、Loewenstein和Weber的工作之后，1990年斯坦福大学的心理学研究生Elizabeth Newton开发了一个实验，现在是知识诅咒的一个经典例子。她要求参与者用手指敲出流行歌曲（被称为 "敲击者"），然后预测这些被敲击的旋律中有多少能被其他人（被称为 "听众"）认出。她还要求敲击者预测有多少人能够正确地猜出旋律。

In a sample of 120 melodies, listeners got it right only 2.5% of the time.<sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">1</sup> The tappers had predicted a _50%_ success rate: they grossly overestimated how well the listeners could guess, due to the curse of knowledge. Once they were given a song to tap, they couldn’t help but hear the melody that their tapping was based on, so they assumed the listeners would _also_ hear the melody.<sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">4</sup> In reality, all the listeners heard was a random series of taps.<sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">1</sup>  
在120个旋律的样本中，听众只有2.5%的时间猜对了。0# 敲击者曾预测过50%的成功率：由于知识的诅咒，他们严重高估了听众的猜测能力。一旦他们得到一首歌曲来敲击，他们就会情不自禁地听到他们敲击所依据的旋律，所以他们认为听众也会听到这个旋律。实际上，听众听到的只是一系列随机的敲击声。 <sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">1</sup>

The curse of knowledge was popularized in the 2007 book, _Made to Stick: Why Some Ideas Survive and Others Die_.<sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">10</sup> Therein, Chip and Dan Heath brothers explore the concept of “stickiness:” making ideas become memorable and interesting. Their claim is that, by making ideas sticky, we can avoid the curse of knowledge: they become so memorable we never forget them in the first place. If our memory of a given choice is really memorable, we would be less prone to remembering and reevaluating that choice in accordance with what we know now.  
知识的诅咒在2007年的书中得到了普及，书名是Made to Stick。为什么有些想法能生存，而有些则会死亡。在书中，奇普和丹-希斯兄弟探讨了 "粘性 "的概念：使想法变得令人难忘和有趣。他们的主张是，通过使想法具有粘性，我们可以避免知识的诅咒：它们变得如此令人难忘，我们首先就不会忘记它们。如果我们对某个选择的记忆真的令人难忘，我们就不容易记住并根据我们现在所知道的情况重新评估这个选择。

## Consequences 后果

Camerer, Loewenstein, and Weber discussed the implications of the curse of knowledge for economics.<sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">3</sup> The first type of situation where the curse of knowledge might be important is in cases of asymmetric information. When people have private information that less informed people lack, the traditional economic assumption is that better informed people, behaving as a _**[homo economicus](https://thedecisionlab.com/reference-guide/economics/homo-economicus)**_, will optimally exploit their informational advantage.  
卡梅勒、洛温斯坦和韦伯讨论了知识的诅咒对经济学的影响。 <sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">3</sup> 知识诅咒可能很重要的第一类情况是在信息不对称的情况下。当人们拥有信息量较小的人所缺乏的私人信息时，传统的经济学假设是，信息量较大的人，作为经济人，会以最佳方式利用他们的信息优势。

However, the study found the opposite. A _homo economicus_ with an information advantage would make the same offer regardless of the monetary amount to be divided–that way, they could maximize their profits. Yet, Camerer, Loewenstein and Weber found that people present a _larger_ offer when the amount to be divided is larger. Well-informed people simply assume everyone has the same information, so they make a fairer offer because they think everyone knows what they know. In this situation of informational imbalance, the curse on knowledge yields a _fairer_ deal.  
然而，该研究发现了相反的情况。拥有信息优势的阿莫经济人，无论要分的钱是多少，都会提出同样的报价--这样，他们就能实现利润最大化。然而，Camerer、Loewenstein和Weber发现，当需要分割的金额较大时，人们会提出更多的报价。信息灵通的人只是假设每个人都有相同的信息，所以他们会提出更公平的报价，因为他们认为每个人都知道他们所知道的东西。在这种信息不平衡的情况下，对知识的诅咒会产生一个公平的交易。

The second situation where the curse of knowledge might be important is when people learn more over time and have to reconstruct their earlier perspectives.<sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">3</sup> Here, the curse of knowledge interacts with another bias (**[hindsight bias](https://thedecisionlab.com/biases/hindsight-bias)**). When we’re evaluating our decisions in the past, we tend to mistakenly assume that we knew then what we know now. So, we think that an unfavorable outcome was known to be unfavorable, and that a favorable outcome was always known to be favorable. The curse of knowledge, along with hindsight bias, leads us to judge _previous_ choices according to our _current_ knowledge, even when we didn’t have that information at the time.  
知识的诅咒可能很重要的第二种情况是，人们随着时间的推移学到了更多的东西，不得不重新构建他们早期的观点。在这里，知识的诅咒与另一种偏见（事后诸葛亮偏见）相互作用。当我们在评估过去的决定时，我们往往会错误地认为我们当时知道的就是现在知道的。因此，我们认为一个不利的结果是已知的，而一个有利的结果总是知道是有利的。知识的诅咒，加上事后诸葛亮式的偏见，导致我们根据我们现在的知识来判断以前的选择，即使我们当时并没有掌握这些信息。

A third situation where the curse of knowledge is at play is in teaching, especially for engineering and science in higher education.<sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">4</sup> While instructors have extensive expertise in their fields, there tends to be a disconnect between what they understand and what their students understand. Some credit this disconnect to instructors’ lack of training and experience in teaching, being distracted by greater interests in research, or simply not caring about their students’ understanding.<sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">5</sup>知识的诅咒发挥作用的第三种情况是在教学中，特别是在高等教育的工程和科学方面。 <sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">4</sup> 虽然教员在他们的领域有广泛的专业知识，但他们的理解和他们的学生的理解之间往往存在着脱节。有些人认为这种脱节是由于导师缺乏培训和教学经验，被更大的研究兴趣所分散，或者根本不关心学生的理解。 <sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">5</sup>  

The curse of knowledge gives us a more straightforward (and more charitable) explanation. While expertise in a field can increase instructors’ confidence in their ability to teach, they struggle to deliver the material in a way that fits what their students know.<sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">6</sup> Cognitive scientist Steve Pinker identified the following issues in how instructors in higher-education deliver content:<sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">7</sup>  
知识的诅咒给了我们一个更直接的（也更慈善的）解释。虽然在某一领域的专业知识可以增加教员对自己教学能力的信心，但他们却很难以符合学生知识的方式来传递材料。认知科学家史蒂夫-平克(Steve Pinker)指出，在高等教育中教员如何传递内容方面存在以下问题。 <sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">7</sup>

-   Abstract language is used for already complex topics;  
    抽象语言用于已经复杂的主题。
-   There can be clumsy transitions between related topics, making the connection between them opaque;  
    相关主题之间可能存在笨拙的过渡，使它们之间的联系变得不透明。
-   Instructors often use so-called zombie nouns instead of verbs or adjectives (verb + _ization,_ e.g., operationalization); and,  
    教员经常使用所谓的僵尸名词，而不是动词或形容词（动词+化，例如，操作化）；以及。
-   There can be inadequate interpretations of external sources, further confusing students.  
    对外部资料的解释可能不充分，使学生更加困惑。

The curse of knowledge also has implications for our everyday lives.<sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">9</sup> Many people write to-do lists to keep themselves organized, but sometimes they can look less like a comprehensive list; and more of a collection of scattered sticky notes. This is because, when we lose or forget one of the items of our to-do list, and then reencounter it months later, the curse of knowledge makes us forget why that needed to be done. So, a to-do list item becomes a pointless series of words scrawled on a page.  
知识的诅咒对我们的日常生活也有影响。许多人写待办事项清单以保持自己的条理性，但有时它们看起来不像是一份全面的清单；而更像是一组散乱的便条。这是因为，当我们丢失或忘记了待办事项清单中的一个项目，然后在几个月后重新遇到它时，知识的诅咒使我们忘记了为什么需要做这件事。因此，待办事项清单上的项目变成了一连串毫无意义的涂鸦。

## Case Study 案例研究

##### **Beating the curse of knowledge 击败知识的诅咒**

Who doesn’t love online shopping? A few clicks here and there, and voila! A pretty package shows up at your door with everything you need – and you didn’t even have to change out of your pajamas. One caveat to the convenience of online shopping, however, are the delivery times. If you’re unlucky, you might have to wait three weeks for a package that was promised to be delivered in one week, or your package might get lost completely. And by that point, the curse of knowledge might kick in: employees and managers might forget which packages went where, because they can no longer remember what it was like to have that old information.  
谁不喜欢网上购物？在这里和那里点击几下，就可以了!一个漂亮的包裹出现在你的门口，里面有你所需要的一切--而你甚至不需要换掉你的睡衣。然而，网上购物的便利性有一个注意事项，那就是交货时间。如果你不走运，你可能不得不为一个承诺在一周内送达的包裹等待三个星期，或者你的包裹可能完全丢失。而到那时，知识的诅咒可能会启动：员工和经理可能会忘记哪些包裹被送到了哪里，因为他们不再记得以前的信息是什么样的。

FedEx recognizes these struggles and avoids the curse of knowledge by telling a convincing story with concrete language.<sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">1</sup> Executive members who developed the FedEx vision could have easily used ambiguous language with no motivational component to reach their goals – but this wasn’t the case here. Rather, FedEx’s leaders avoided the curse of knowledge by translating their strategies into actionable language: packages are promised to “absolutely, positively” arrive overnight. This ensures that the promised deadline becomes “sticky,” thereby ensuring that FedEx’s promises turn into actions. Sticky words become actions. FedEx also incentives employees away from the curse of knowledge by awarding a Purple Promise award to people who uphold the company’s guaranteed delivery time.  
联邦快递认识到了这些斗争，并通过用具体的语言讲述一个令人信服的故事来避免知识的诅咒。 <sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">1</sup> 制定联邦快递愿景的执行成员可以很容易地使用没有激励成分的模糊语言来实现他们的目标--但这里的情况并非如此。相反，联邦快递的领导人通过将他们的战略转化为可操作的语言，避免了知识的诅咒：包裹被承诺 "绝对、肯定 "在一夜之间到达。这确保了承诺的最后期限成为 "粘性"，从而确保联邦快递的承诺变成了行动。粘性语言变成了行动。联邦快递还通过向坚持公司保证交货时间的人颁发紫色承诺奖，激励员工远离知识的诅咒。

##### **Faculty development strategies 教师发展战略**

In light of the discussion around the curse of knowledge in higher education, researchers have explored potential strategies to increase awareness of this cognitive bias among instructors, and close the teacher-student gap.<sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">4</sup> In line with the Heath brothers’ suggestion that sticky messages can be used to combat the curse of knowledge,10 the following principles have been suggested for instructors:<sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">4</sup>  
鉴于高等教育中围绕知识诅咒的讨论，研究人员探讨了潜在的策略，以提高教员对这种认知偏见的认识，并缩小师生差距。根据Heath兄弟的建议，粘性信息可以用来对抗知识的诅咒10，以下原则被建议给教员。 <sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">4</sup>

-   **Simplicity:** Section out content and distill it into concise, accessible messages.  
    简洁性:对内容进行分割，并将其提炼成简明、易懂的信息。
-   **Unexpectedness:** Step beyond the mindset that since you are curious about your content, your students will be too. Instead, actively share opportunities for curiosity!  
    出乎意料：不要以为你对你的内容有好奇心，你的学生也会有。相反，要积极分享好奇心的机会!
-   **Concreteness:** Provide and ask students to provide concrete, specific examples.  
    具体性:提供并要求学生提供具体的、特定的例子。
-   **Credibility:** Use problem- or project-based learning to engage students with issues related to the content. They may want to learn more about a topic once they’ve engaged with it.  
    公信力：使用基于问题或项目的学习，让学生参与到与内容相关的问题中。一旦他们参与了某一主题，他们可能想要了解更多。
-   **Emotions:** Allow room to cope with the emotional frustration that can be part of learning.  
    情感：为应对学习中可能出现的情感挫折留出空间。
-   **Stories:** Use stories to make content more personal and accessible to students. Stories are also great memory aids!  
    故事：用故事来使内容更加个人化，更容易被学生接受。故事也是很好的记忆辅助工具!

On top of these six principles, researchers suggest that instructors evaluate students’ mindsets at the beginning of course, to better understand what they know initially.<sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">4</sup>  Researchers also suggest collecting feedback throughout the course, to make sure students can convey their feelings, experiences, and stories. Gathering this feedback can inform successful teaching practice, fostering student-teacher connections that make it easier for teachers to understand their students’ perspective. It might even lead to better grades!  
在这六条原则的基础上，研究人员建议教员在课程开始时评估学生的心态，以更好地了解他们最初的认识。 <sup data-immersive-translate-effect="1">4</sup> 研究人员还建议在整个课程中收集反馈，以确保学生能够传达他们的感受、经验和故事。收集这些反馈可以为成功的教学实践提供信息，促进师生之间的联系，使教师更容易理解学生的观点。这甚至可能导致更好的成绩!
